The Edinburgh Film Festival

The Edinburgh International Film Festival (EIFF), founded in 1947, is the world's oldest continually running film festival and remains a cornerstone of global cinema culture. Held annually in August, the festival showcases an eclectic mix of world premieres, UK debuts, documentaries, shorts, and experimental works, alongside retrospectives that celebrate film history. Known for its intimate yet ambitious approach, EIFF has introduced audiences to groundbreaking talent and hosted iconic premieres such as Blade Runner, Alien, and The Hurt Locker. Beyond screenings, the festival features industry panels, Q&A sessions, and networking events, attracting filmmakers, critics, and enthusiasts from around the world to Edinburgh's vibrant cultural scene
